How do investments work?

  1. An issuer, usually a company, issues securities to be traded on the market (the exchange).
  2. A regulatory, body will oversee the trading system to prevent fraud and embezzlement.
  3. A facilitor, in this case the stock exchange, will ensure that the transaction between the issuer and the investor is safe, fair, transparent and orderly.
  4. Finally, the inversor, which is usually a person or a company, invests in securities on the market to make a profit in return, such as money or shares.

Futures

Futures is a hedging instrument that allows the person who uses it, to secure the value of their assets at a later time. These instruments are considered "derivatives" because their price will be subject to the value of another instrument, called an "underlying asset".

Binary Options

Binary options are Over The Counter derivative instruments that are not traded in an organized market. Binary Options is based on predicting how the price of a given financial instrument will behave after a period of time, i.e. whether it will go up or down.

Forex

Forex, also known as Forex, FX or Forex Trading, is a decentralized world market of all currencies traded around the world. This market is the largest and most liquid in the world, with a daily trading volume exceeding $5 trillion.This is the most profitable market.
